Why Terms and Conditions Are Crucial for Your Business

There is always a lot to do when you are trying to run your own business, so terms and conditions usually fall to the bottom of the priority list. Whether you are a start-up or an established business, now more than ever, a robust set of terms and conditions is essential. They avoid any misunderstanding about what you are selling and the terms on which you are selling it.

Your terms and conditions establish a legally binding contract between you and your clients. It is important that your terms and conditions are written specifically for your business. No two businesses are the same, and another business may not have the same needs as your own.

Benefits of Having Clear Terms and Conditions

They clearly set out the key terms which govern the parties to the contract and help both parties understand their duties, rights, roles, and responsibilities. Having a clear set of terms and conditions means businesses can maintain a consistent approach whilst also delivering good customer service.

How to Avoid Common Errors in Business Terms and Conditions

Suppliers often ask customers to sign their terms and conditions for speed and ease in today’s busy business world. Using terms and conditions can streamline contract negotiations and make trading more manageable by applying the same standards for all customers. However, there are various pitfalls to watch out for when using business terms and conditions. Making common errors could put your company at risk of harm. This article will explore avoiding common errors for suppliers to avoid in business terms and conditions.

Benefits of Having Clear Terms and Conditions

They clearly set out the key terms which govern the parties to the contract and help both parties understand their duties, rights, roles, and responsibilities. Having a clear set of terms and conditions means businesses can maintain a consistent approach whilst also delivering good customer service.

Common Issues with Terms and Conditions

1. Ambiguous or Overly Broad Clauses

Example: Facebook's T&Cs have sometimes included vague language about how user data could be used, leading to user confusion and criticism. In some cases, this resulted in accusations of overreach regarding data sharing.

Impact: Lack of clarity can erode trust and prompt legal challenges.

2. Unenforceable or Illegal Terms

Example: Many companies attempt to limit liability or restrict users' rights in ways that violate local laws. In 2020, Amazon faced criticism for terms that sought to limit consumer warranty rights, which were unenforceable in certain jurisdictions.

Impact: Courts may strike down these clauses, and companies might face fines.

3. Failure to Notify Users of Changes

Example: In 2012, Instagram changed its T&Cs to include clauses about potentially using user photos in advertisements without explicit consent. Users were not properly notified of this change, leading to backlash and a retraction.

Impact: Users feel blindsided, leading to reputational damage.

4. Privacy Violations

Example: Google’s T&Cs for Gmail were criticized for scanning email content to target ads. Although disclosed in their terms, many users felt this practice was invasive and not clearly explained.

Impact: Breaches of privacy laws like GDPR or CCPA can lead to massive fines.

5. Inadequate User Protection

Example: Uber’s arbitration clause in its T&Cs made it difficult for users to sue the company in court, even in cases of serious issues like data breaches or safety concerns. This was heavily criticized for prioritizing corporate interests over user rights.

Impact: Public outcry and potential regulatory intervention.

6. Misleading Terms

Example: In 2014, LinkedIn faced a class-action lawsuit for “misleading” users into giving access to their email contacts, which were then spammed with invitations.

Impact: Loss of user trust and legal settlements.

7. Ignoring Accessibility Needs

Example: Some companies publish T&Cs in legal jargon or small fonts, making them inaccessible to users with disabilities or those who speak a different native language.

Impact: Potential violations of accessibility laws, such as the ADA.

8. Unrealistic or Onerous Requirements

Example: Blizzard Entertainment required users to waive certain rights when using its Battle.net gaming service, leading to complaints about unfair treatment.

Impact: Reduced goodwill and possible changes forced by regulators.

Lessons for Companies

  • Transparency: Use plain language and highlight major changes.
  • Compliance: Ensure terms adhere to regional laws and regulations.
  • User Feedback: Engage users when drafting or revising terms.
  • Accountability: Regular audits of terms to prevent potential issues.
